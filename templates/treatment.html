Treatment Options

  • Basic treatments help maintain the health and well-being of your hens. Always consult a vet for severe or persistent issues.
Parasite Control
External Parasites (Mites & Lice)
Regular dusting with poultry-safe powders or sprays helps control mites and lice. Use diatomaceous earth in the coop and nesting boxes.
Internal Parasites (Worms)
Dewormers such as fenbendazole or ivermectin should be used every 3-6 months. Always follow recommended dosages.
Wound Care
Cleaning
For minor wounds, clean with a saline solution or mild antiseptic.
Treatment
Apply a poultry-safe wound spray or antibiotic ointment to the affected area.
Isolation
If needed, isolate the injured hen to prevent pecking by others.
Respiratory Issues
Symptoms
Sneezing, coughing, nasal discharge, or labored breathing can indicate respiratory infections.
Treatment
Use antibiotics if prescribed by a vet. Keep the coop well-ventilated to prevent respiratory issues.
Egg Binding
Symptoms
Hens may appear lethargic, have difficulty walking, or strain without laying.
Treatment
Gently massage the abdomen and offer a warm bath to help the hen relax. In severe cases, consult a vet.
Prolapsed Vent
Symptoms
Prolapse occurs when part of the oviduct is exposed after laying an egg.
Treatment
Gently clean the area, apply a lubricant, and carefully push the tissue back inside. Isolate the hen and ensure proper nutrition.
Molting Support
Symptoms
Hens lose feathers and may reduce egg-laying during molting.
Treatment
Provide extra protein in their diet to support feather regrowth during this natural process.
Coccidiosis Prevention
Symptoms
Diarrhea, lethargy, and weight loss are signs of coccidiosis.
Treatment
Use a medicated feed or add anti-coccidial drugs like amprolium to water.
Foot Problems (Bumblefoot)
Symptoms
Swelling or scabs on the bottom of the foot.
Treatment
Clean the area, apply antiseptic, and bandage the foot. In severe cases, consult a vet.
Vaccination
Tip :-
Vaccinating helps prevent common diseases like Marek's disease, Newcastle disease, and Infectious Bronchitis.
Nutritional Supplement
Tip :-
Provide grit and calcium supplements to promote digestion and strong eggshells. Vitamins can boost the immune system.

Detect which disease is affecting your hens to ensure proper treatment. Take the necessary steps by identifying symptoms and applying the right solution.
